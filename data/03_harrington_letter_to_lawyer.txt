October 11, 1987

Mr. Gerald Oakes
Oakes & Tillman, Attorneys at Law
Suite 400, 22 Federal Street
Boston, MA 02110

Gerald,

I am writing to you in confidence and ask that you treat this letter as privileged
communication. I intend to speak with you in person early next week, but I want
a written record of my concerns established before that meeting.

Over the past six weeks I have been reviewing the Q3 financial statements in
preparation for the annual board presentation. During this review I discovered
a series of payments totalling $2.3 million made over the past 18 months to an
external consultancy called Alpine Consulting LLC. I do not recognise this vendor
and it does not appear in any of our approved supplier lists prior to early 1986.

When I asked our comptroller, Robert Staines, for the underlying contracts and
deliverables for Alpine Consulting, he told me he had forwarded my request to
Edmund Price's office. That was eleven days ago. I have received nothing. When I
raised the matter directly with Edmund last week, he told me the payments were for
"strategic advisory services" related to our new compound pipeline and that the
documentation was "in order." He did not provide the documentation.

I have worked with Edmund for nine years. I do not wish to make an accusation
I cannot substantiate. But I believe there is something seriously wrong here and
I want your guidance on my legal obligations — to the board, to the shareholders,
and potentially to regulatory bodies.

I have photocopied the relevant audit pages and will bring them to our meeting.
I have also noted two transfers in September that originated from the company's
R&D reserve account, which requires dual executive authorisation. I do not
recall co-signing those transfers.

Please advise whether I should approach the board audit committee before speaking
with you, or whether I should wait.

Yours sincerely,

Victor Harrington
CEO, Harrington Pharmaceutical Inc.

P.S. — I am also troubled by the circumstances of the April laboratory accident.
The primary loss was the physical evidence log for our Batch 7734-X synthesis
trials. I did not think much of it at the time, but in retrospect the timing
strikes me as worth noting. The log was the only non-digitised record of that
compound's formulation history.
