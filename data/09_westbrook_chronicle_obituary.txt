HARRINGTON PHARMACEUTICAL FOUNDER AND CEO DIES AT 46
Company Flags "Unexplained Circumstances"; Police Open Inquiry

By Staff Reporter Caroline Fitch

Victor James Harrington, the founder and chief executive of Harrington
Pharmaceutical Inc., died Tuesday evening at the company's headquarters on
Commerce Parkway, Westbrook. He was 46 years old.

Mr. Harrington built Harrington Pharmaceutical from a two-person research
partnership in 1971 into one of the region's foremost mid-sized pharmaceutical
companies, employing more than 340 people and holding patents on several
compounds currently in clinical trials.

A company spokesperson confirmed that Mr. Harrington was found unresponsive
in his office shortly before 7 p.m. on Tuesday. Emergency services were called
immediately. The cause of death has not been publicly disclosed pending results
of a toxicology examination, which police say may take several weeks.

Detective Sergeant Ronald Marsh of Westbrook Police told the Chronicle that
the death is being treated as unexplained and that a formal investigation has
been opened. "We are not ruling anything out at this stage," Det. Marsh said.
"We ask anyone with relevant information to contact the department."

Edmund Price, the company's Chief Financial Officer, released a brief statement
on behalf of the board on Wednesday. "Victor was the heart of this company.
We are in shock and deeply saddened. The board will ensure the company continues
to operate with the same integrity and vision Victor embodied. We are fully
cooperating with police."

Mr. Price has assumed interim executive authority pending a formal succession
decision by the board, according to a company filing submitted to the
Massachusetts Secretary of State's office on Wednesday morning.

Victor Harrington is survived by his wife, Eleanor, and two daughters,
Catherine (19) and Ruth (16). A private memorial service will be held this
weekend. A public service will be announced.

HARRINGTON PHARMACEUTICAL: A BRIEF HISTORY

Founded 1971 by Victor Harrington and research partner Dr. Alan Frost.
The company's flagship compound, HP-114, received FDA approval in 1981.
Revenue for fiscal year 1986 was reported at $58 million.
The company's most promising pipeline asset is Compound 7734-X, currently
in late preclinical stages, described by analysts as a potential blockbuster
in its target therapeutic area.
