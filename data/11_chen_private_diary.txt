Personal diary of Maria Elena Chen
[Found by Det. Marsh during warrant search of Chen residence — Nov 3, 1987]
[Entry dated April 10, 1987]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

April 10, 1987

Something strange today. I stayed late to finish the column assay notes —
probably 6:30 or so — and as I was coming back from the vending machine
on the second floor I heard voices from inside Lab B. Lab B is supposed to
be locked after 6pm; Dr. Frost is very strict about it.

The door was slightly open. I didn't mean to eavesdrop but I heard Mr. Price's
voice — I recognised it immediately. He was saying something about "the log
needs to be gone before Frost gets back from the conference." Then another
voice, someone I didn't know, said "we can't have the chain of custody
documented anywhere." Mr. Price replied that he "would handle it tonight."

I looked through the gap in the door. There was a second man — tall, grey
suit, maybe 50s, someone I'd never seen in the building before. He was
carrying a leather briefcase. He handed Mr. Price what looked like a thick
envelope.

I backed away quickly and took the stairs. I don't think they saw me.

I don't know what to do with this. The "log" — they must mean the synthesis
log for Batch 7734. The one that Dr. Frost keeps locked in the fireproof
cabinet. Why would Mr. Price want that gone?

The man with the briefcase felt wrong. He didn't look like anyone who
works here. I keep thinking about the envelope he handed over.

I'm going to wait and see if anything happens. Maybe I misheard.

[Entry dated April 12, 1987]

There was a fire in Lab B yesterday evening. The synthesis log for 7734 is gone.
Mr. Price reviewed the accident report himself.

I don't know what to do. I'm scared.

[Entry dated April 18, 1987]

Mr. Price called me into his office today. He said he heard I'd been working
late on the 10th and asked if I'd noticed anything "unusual" around the labs.

I said no. I was too frightened to say anything else.

He thanked me and said the company "appreciated discretion." Then he told me
he had approved an "exceptional performance bonus" for me of $4,000, effective
immediately. He said it was for my "dedication and professionalism."

I haven't told anyone. I don't know who I could tell.

I keep thinking about that man with the grey suit and the briefcase.
I looked it up in the visitor log. April 10th. There was no visitor logged
for Lab B that evening. No one signed in from outside.

Whoever he was, he was never officially there.
