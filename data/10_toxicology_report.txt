Specimen A (Blood):
  Digoxin concentration: 8.4 ng/mL
  [Reference: therapeutic range 0.8–2.0 ng/mL; toxic threshold >3.0 ng/mL]
  Ethanol: not detected
  Standard drug panel: negative for opioids, benzodiazepines, barbiturates,
  amphetamines, cocaine metabolites.

Specimen C (Vitreous humor):
  Digoxin concentration: 7.1 ng/mL — consistent with ante-mortem accumulation
  rather than post-mortem redistribution. Indicates exposure occurred hours
  before death, not immediately prior.

Specimen D (Cup 1 — decedent's):
  Digoxin detected: 0.34 mg/mL dissolved in beverage matrix.
  At estimated cup volume (180 mL) and partial consumption: estimated ingested
  dose approximately 28–35 mg. A fatal oral dose of digoxin in an adult of
  Mr. Harrington's weight (estimated 82 kg) is approximately 10–20 mg.

Specimen E (Cup 2 — visitor's):
  Digoxin: not detected.
  No other substances detected.

Specimen F (Gastric):
  Digoxin present, consistent with oral ingestion. Partial digestion state
  consistent with ingestion 90–150 minutes before death.

CAUSE AND MANNER OF DEATH
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CAUSE OF DEATH   : Acute digoxin toxicity — cardiac arrhythmia (ventricular
                   fibrillation) secondary to intentional or accidental digoxin
                   overdose.

MANNER OF DEATH  : HOMICIDE

NOTES: The level of digoxin found in the decedent's blood exceeds any plausible
therapeutic or accidental self-administration scenario. The decedent had no known
cardiac condition and was not prescribed digoxin. Digoxin was detected exclusively
in Cup 1 (the cup from which the decedent drank) and not in Cup 2 (the visitor's
cup), confirming selective administration to the decedent.

Ingestion timing (90–150 minutes pre-mortem) places administration between
approximately 17:10 and 17:30 — consistent with a meeting at 17:00.
