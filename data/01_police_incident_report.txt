SUMMARY OF EVENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
At approximately 18:42 on October 14, 1987, emergency services were dispatched
to the executive floor of Harrington Pharmaceutical Inc., 900 Commerce Parkway,
Westbrook, MA, following a 911 call placed by receptionist Donna Hayward.

Upon arrival, paramedics found Mr. Victor Harrington unresponsive in his private
office. Resuscitation was attempted for 22 minutes without success. Mr. Harrington
was pronounced dead at the scene at 19:04.

The decedent had been present in the building since approximately 08:15 that
morning, according to the lobby security log. His personal assistant, Ms. Patricia
Wren, confirmed that Mr. Harrington had scheduled a private meeting in his office
at 17:00 with no name recorded in the calendar — Mr. Harrington had personally
blocked the time and instructed Ms. Wren not to disturb him.

Ms. Wren left the building at 17:30. She states she heard no unusual sounds from
the office. The building's main reception closed at 18:00. Ms. Hayward, the evening
receptionist, heard a "heavy thud" from the direction of the executive corridor at
approximately 18:40 and, upon investigating, found Mr. Harrington collapsed beside
his desk.
