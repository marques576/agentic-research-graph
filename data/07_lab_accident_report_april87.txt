HARRINGTON PHARMACEUTICAL INC.
INTERNAL SAFETY INVESTIGATION REPORT

Incident Type   : Laboratory Fire / Chemical Accident
Report Number   : SAF-87-009
Date of Incident: April 11, 1987  (approximately 19:30)
Location        : Research Lab B, 3rd Floor, 900 Commerce Parkway, Westbrook MA
Prepared by     : James Holloway, Health & Safety Officer
Reviewed by     : Edmund Price, CFO (acting in absence of CEO, who was travelling)
Report Date     : April 18, 1987

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
At approximately 19:30 on April 11, 1987, a fire alarm was activated on the
third floor research wing. Westbrook Fire Department (Engine 4) responded within
six minutes. A solvent fire had started in Lab B, originating near the chemical
storage cabinet on the east wall. The fire was contained to approximately 4 square
metres before suppression. No injuries were reported.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CAUSE DETERMINATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Investigation concluded the fire was caused by improper storage of diethyl ether
in proximity to an electrical panel. A loose wire in the panel is believed to
have produced a spark. This finding is consistent with fire department report
FD-87-041. Building management has since replaced the electrical panel (completed
April 25, 1987).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PROPERTY LOSS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The following items were destroyed and are not recoverable:

  - Approximately $14,000 in laboratory equipment (spectrometer components,
    pipettes, storage containers)
  - The physical Evidence and Synthesis Log for Batch 7734-X, covering the
    full formulation history of Compound 7734-X from initial synthesis
    (October 1984) through March 1987. This was the sole physical copy.
    Digital records for this batch were stored on the Lab B workstation,
    which sustained water damage from fire suppression and could not be
    recovered. IT Services confirmed data loss on April 14, 1987.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
ANOMALIES NOTED (for internal record)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  - The building security log shows Lab B was accessed at 18:47 on April 11
    using badge ID EP-001. Badge EP-001 is registered to Edmund Price, CFO.
    Mr. Price is not assigned to the research division and does not typically
    access Lab B. When asked, Mr. Price stated he had entered briefly to
    retrieve a document left by a colleague. No corroboration was sought.

  - The fire started approximately 43 minutes after the badge access.

  - The Evidence and Synthesis Log for Batch 7734-X was stored in a fireproof
    cabinet at the time of incident. The cabinet was found open after the fire.
    The lock mechanism showed no signs of forced entry. The key to this cabinet
    is ordinarily held by the Lab B supervisor (Dr. Alan Frost) and a spare
    kept in the H&S office. The spare key was found in the H&S key box.
    Dr. Frost's key was on his person (he was not in the building that evening).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CONCLUSION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Incident classified as accidental. Remedial works completed. Case closed.
